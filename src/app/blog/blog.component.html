8 Quick tips For Students to learn programming online

Posted By: Urooj Murtaza

How does a computer perform calculations, scan your system, and search the web in a blink of an eye? Well, that’s possible due to something we call “programming”.

What is programming?

Programming is the act of making programs, software, computer applications, websites, portals, etc. Through programming a service can be attained from the computer. From windows to Facebook, from editing software to games all that we see on screen has been developed by a genius with programming skills. Our computers are able to work through the programs we feed into them. It’s like instructions you give to your employee so he/she can work as you desire. E.g. Online Steam (Steam is a gaming platform) works with the help of programmers adding, replacing and managing its features. From games to simple alarm apps it’s the programmers that make it possible.

8 Quick tips to get started

If you want to learn to program online, then here are 8 tips to get started. Regardless of you being a freshman or an experienced pro, it’s always great to learn more than what you already know.
Understand why are you learning programming
Before you start learning anything it's important you know the reason. Whether you want to makes games, apps, websites, or operating systems it's important you know what you are aiming for and then consult people in that field to grow. There are people who you can approach online and join groups to get more help. With the right mindset, you will know exactly what you want at the end and there will be fewer uncertainties. Choose your path wisely and then start working towards it.
Start from the very basics
As with all skills, it’s important in programming as well that you have a clear understanding of the basic concepts. If you develop problem-solving abilities and know how codes work at the back end then you will find it easier to learn further. The beginning is harder for everyone but once you make it through this phase you will develop interest as you go ahead. Don’t skip steps as you will find yourself stuck at many times and won’t know how to get out of it.
Start with the right language
As a beginner, you should learn a low-level language because it will serve as a great introduction about how programming works in general. C++ is a low–level language that can help you learn how programs work and how it makes your computer carry out a task. The language you begin with should be basic enough to help you understand what programming is all about. Check online articles to find the best language for you.
Write code by hand
It sounds funny that you should write programs by hand but it can help you: Remember things better Generate code from your brain rather than from Google Understand errors better You won’t get much help about errors as well so that will help you focus more on writing error-free on your own.
Use online resources
There are many platforms like: Edx Coursera Udemy etc. You can take courses and learn from teachers at popular universities around the globe. The courses are designed for different levels and many offer certificates that you can show on your CV.
Ask professionals
If you come across a professional working in the same field as you and you feel like you could use a little help then do reach out. Many people who are at the top were once beginners like you so they will be happy to help you out. Build your connections, take guidance and explore more people to expand your knowledge. Email them, contribute to codes online and take criticism seriously.
Practice as much as possible
As the saying goes ‘Practice makes Perfect’, this is one of the oldest but useful pieces of advice. Practice as much as you can till you think you can teach it to someone. Learning, practicing, and teaching will greatly help you become a better programmer.
Try to learn from your mistakes
As a programmer you will make countless mistakes, you will run into problems, and will stay stuck for days. That’s natural and is what every programmer goes through. The mistakes are a part of your learning but make sure you know the reason why the issues occurred so it can be avoided and you don’t end up making the same mistake again.

Conclusion

In this article, we learned what programming is, and 8 tips you can follow to learn to program online. These tips are generic and work for everyone but with more research, you will know more ways that work best for you. Keep working hard, stay focused, don’t worry too much, and eventually, you will get there.

How to Wash a Hammock

Posted By: Urooj Murtaza

While chilling on a hammock is pretty relaxing, cleaning it can seem a bit of a task. After using your hammock for quite some time, it becomes dirty and inevitably you have to clean it! While you wouldn’t want to ruin it, you do want to clean it fast right? If that is what you want, you are in the right place. In this article, we will share some easy and useful tips with you to wash a hammock. Once you go through these tips, washing a hammock will become a piece of cake!!

The 4-steps to washing a hammock


Here are 4 simple steps you need to take to wash your hammock.
Analyze the material and the damage
Firstly, you need to know the material. Different materials are washed differently so check the manufacturer's guidelines. Secondly, you should check for stains, mild dew, and any damages to the fabric. If the fabric is ripped from any side or if there is any other type of damage then repair that first. Afterwards, check for stains. These two steps will help you decide the method for washing and understand how much you need to clean.
Remove the carabiners and spreader-bars
After you are done analyzing the hammock, you must separate the carabiners or any other metallic components. If you skip this step you will end up with rust due to the moisture interacting with the metal. Pieces of wood also get affected by moisture so separate all extra parts and put them in a safe place. If the spreader bars are not removable then you should keep them away from water while washing.
Wash it up!!
Finally, let’s move on to the cleaning part. Depending on the material and the manufacturer's guide you can choose to soak, machine wash or use a hose to clean the hammock. Whatever way you choose to follow don't put any fabric softeners, any other fabric, or bleach at all while washing!!!
Tub Wash:
Add cold water, your hammock, and a mild detergent in a tub, and let it sit for some time. For a rope hammock, tie the two ends to avoid the ropes from tangling. Rub the ropes by hand to remove any stains. After an hour remove the water and replace it with clean water. Keep adding water till it becomes clear. If your hammock has O-rings or non-removable spreaders then keep them away from water while washing. Do wash the O-rings and spreaders a little bit with soap to remove any dirt.
Machine Wash:
If your hammock allows you, go for the laziest way to clean your hammock. In a washing machine, wash the hammock on a delicate-wash mode. Make sure to use a mild detergent and avoid using a dryer as it can damage the fabric.
Hose wash
If you are using a hose to wash your hammock then put it on a concrete slab or any other clean surface. Spray the hammock with water and put some detergent over it or use soapy water. Once the hammock looks cleaned up, rub the fabric with your hands to get rid of dirt. Afterwards, spray water to remove the soap.
Meta Description
So you have a dirty hammock but not a lot of time. What should you do? Follow these 4 simple steps and you are good to go!!

How to Sew Denim at Home

Posted By: Urooj Murtaza

Elio Fiorucci (an Italian fashion designer) once said: “Denim is a love that never fades away,” and we couldn’t agree more! Denim products and especially jeans are evergreen clothing items that never seem to go out of fashion. Denim jeans are trendy, cool, pretty, cute, and beautiful all at the same time. While it’s a very versatile item, we can’t ignore the fact that it can be a bit pricy! Top brands sell it at prices that aren’t affordable to many. To solve that issue, we are here to guide you on how you can stitch your own denim jeans at home and save money! While it may seem intimidating, if you learn to do it properly, you will be able to make that perfect piece that’s just for you!

Steps to Sew denim jeans 600

Below are 6 easy to follow steps that can help anyone stitch denim perfectly.
Design your jeans

Firstly, you need to have a vision of what you want. If you start out without a plan you may end up failing and making something you don’t like. Bad idea! So before you do anything start out with a basic sketch of what you want. You can take inspiration from Pinterest, Google or make something completely original! Make sure the design you choose flatters your body. While you make the design include measurements and select a fabric that goes well with it.

Decide the fabric

Every fabric is not appropriate for every design. For that matter, try to understand what kind of denim is the right choice before leaving for the store. Denim is usually differentiated in terms of weight from 8 to 16 ounces. You can choose the right weight for you which suits your needs. In terms of appearance, denim comes in the following types:
  1. Raw denim: untreated and unwashed
  2. Sanforized denim: soft textured, washed denim
  3. Crushed denim: permanently crushed look with velvet-like weave
  4. Stretched: mix of cotton and spandex or other materials, stretchy
  5. Selvedge denim: fringe at the end
  6. Acid wash denim: washed in acid, has spots and smears
  7. Poly Denim: mix of polyester, cotton, and other fibers

Buy materials to sow

Before you start, you need to have supplies to sew the jeans. The raw materials depend on the style you are going after but generally, the supplies you need include:
  1. Denim fabric (1.6-3.4 meters)/li>
  2. Needles, threads of different thicknesses (several hundred meters in length) depending on the design
  3. Rivets (6-11)
  4. Buttons (1-5) and a zipper
  5. Material for lining
  6. Labels to make things trendy
  7. Scissors and a sewing machine

Take Measurements

Now comes the fun part! After all the planning and shopping are done, it’s time to start taking measurements. A great idea for beginners is to take an old jeans and cut out all its stitching to separate all the components. The pockets, front and back leg, belt, etc. Iron all pieces to flatten them and cut the new components according to the measurements of the old pieces. You can adjust according to the design you want. Simple!

Shrink and Pre-wash

The last step before stitching is washing your fabric and shrinking it. This step is important for all kinds of stitching as fabrics always shrink after washing. If you have denim that’s dark; you can get the colorfast process done, wash it twice, or keep it soaked in vinegar with warm water.

Start sewing

Now comes the tough part. The actual sewing! You can follow a stitching pattern online for more details but mainly you need to do the following steps:
  1. Add any embellishment you want and sew the back pockets on the back leg
  2. Sew the back leg to the back yokes

SBP Sets New Leasing Policy for Automobiles – Limiting the Max Loan to 3 Millions Posted By: Urooj Murtaza

On September 24th, 2021, the State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) revised the prudential regulations (PRS) to tackle rapid loan growth. A rise in imports has been observed, due to higher demands. This has led to an account deficit of 81% month-on-month last August. The revisions in prudential regulations aim to balance the demand growth that will result in a slower import rise, thus stabilizing the “balance-of-payments.” The revisions prohibit the financing of imported vehicles while limiting it for locally manufactured vehicles with an engine capacity greater than 1000cc. Limitations were also imposed on credit cards and personal loans. The new regulations focus mainly on the automobile sector. The SBP has also shortened the duration of auto finance from a maximum tenure of 7 years to 5 years. In the same way, the maximum tenure has been shortened from 5 to 4 years for personal loans. On top of that, the overall limit of auto finance for a person from all banks/DFI has been set to PRK 3000000. The minimum down payment for auto finance has been set to 30% compared to 15% previously.
